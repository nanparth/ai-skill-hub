# Interview prep workflow

Profession-agnostic. Bundled rendering scripts and DOCX templates are currently law-specific (see assets); workflow logic transfers to any profession (engineering, consulting, research, design, finance, government, …).

⚠️ STUB. Placeholder scaffold. Develop when next interview-prep task arrives and lessons captured first-hand.

## When to load

- User asks to prep for upcoming interview
- User has interviewer details, posting, wants practice rounds
- User wants to map STAR narratives to likely questions

## Inputs ⛔ BLOCKING

- Job posting (link, file, or pasted text)
- Interview format (panel, technical, behavioural, case, mixed)
- Interview date and duration
- Interviewer names if known (link to `/intel-person` notes under `./outputs/intel-person/`)
- Any pre-interview materials from employer

## Workflow (to be detailed)

### Stage 1: Posting and company research

- Re-read posting and Major Responsibilities
- Pull employer intel from `./outputs/intel-org/` if available
- Identify role context: key clients or users, recent work, regulatory or technical exposure, commercial or operational pressures

### Stage 2: STAR narrative selection

- Read `./job-applications/assets/content-bank.md#star-narratives`
- Select 5-7 STAR narratives most likely to surface for this role
- Tag each STAR with question categories it answers

### Stage 3: Likely-questions brainstorm

- Technical/substantive (subject-matter depth)
- Behavioural (conflict, judgment, leadership, collaboration)
- Role-specific (why this employer, why this team or practice, why now)
- Case (fact pattern, hypothetical, exercise) if applicable

### Stage 4: Practice rounds

- Rehearse answers to selected questions
- Time each (target 90 seconds to two minutes for behavioural)
- Refine STAR delivery for clarity and signal density
- Iterate on weak answers

### Stage 5: Day-of checklist

- Logistics (location, time, route, contact)
- What to carry (printed CV, portfolio, IDs, notebook)
- Pre-interview ritual
- Follow-up plan (thank-you note timing, named-mention details)
